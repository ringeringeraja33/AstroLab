# Houses as Questions

A reframing adapted from the Chinese psychological astrologer **dogcatcher**
(2012): the twelve houses are not twelve "life areas" to memorize — they are
twelve questions a person keeps asking. Most of a life is spent striving,
wandering, or stuck inside these twelve questions. This file turns that idea
into a working tool for transit interpretation.

## The twelve questions

| House | The question |
|---|---|
| 1  | Who am I? |
| 2  | Am I worth something? Is this worth doing? |
| 3  | What's around here? What's going on? |
| 4  | Where do I belong? Am I at peace? |
| 5  | Is this fun? Am I enjoying it? |
| 6  | Did I finish the task? Am I skilled yet? Was I useful? |
| 7  | Who is with me? How do I face them? |
| 8  | What do others have and want? What will I undergo? |
| 9  | What lies far away? Where are truth, freedom, and the dream? |
| 10 | Am I succeeding? Am I in command of the situation? |
| 11 | Am I seen and appreciated? Are my wishes coming true? |
| 12 | Should I withdraw behind the scenes? Why do I still care? |

## Reading a natal chart with the questions

Four observation layers (also from dogcatcher's framework):

1. **Planets in a house** — the person's characteristic *answer* and attitude
   toward that question. Each planet is a psychological drive, so a house's
   occupants show what kind of energy gets spent on its question.
2. **The house ruler's placement** — *where the person goes looking for the
   answer*. Ruler of the 10th in the 6th: they seek "am I succeeding?"
   through daily craft and being useful. This is often the single most
   practical interpretive move the framework offers.
3. **The ruler's condition** (dignity, strength, influence over other
   rulers) — *how much weight* the person gives that question relative to
   the others.
4. **Links between rulers** (reception, mutual reception, aspects) — how
   questions chain together: answering one becomes the person's route to
   another.

## The endowment hypothesis

The source presents this as an interpretive hypothesis, not a measured fact.
Here, *endowment* means a constructive quality or a person's potential to
build a satisfying life. It may show up as a physical trait, a psychological
capacity, outside support, or something developed later through practice.

Keep two layers separate:

1. **Basic endowments** come from the luminaries and visible
   planets. They describe psychological functions and the qualities available
   to the person.
2. **Upper endowments** come from houses and angles. They describe
   where those qualities seek a route, what motivates their use, and which
   life questions receive more weight.

This distinction prevents a common reading error: a house does not supply a
trait by itself, and a planet does not describe the route by itself. Read the
planet as a capacity, then read its house, rulerships, and links as the route
through which that capacity is sought and expressed.

### Basic endowments carried by planets

| Factor | Constructive capacity |
|---|---|
| Sun | vitality, initiative, being seen, dignity |
| Moon | warmth, care, safety, belonging |
| Mercury | precision, detail, fluency, agility |
| Venus | harmony, beauty, comfort, pleasure |
| Mars | will, force, speed, directed action |
| Jupiter | breadth, freedom, foresight, uplift |
| Saturn | maturity, reliability, order, necessary control |

The source groups Sun and Moon around vitality and emotional nourishment,
Venus and Jupiter around easier or more expansive experience, and Mercury and
Saturn around effort, tension, and refinement. Treat these pairings as an
interpretive aid rather than a scoring system.

The PDF draws a boundary around the outer planets: Uranus, Neptune, and Pluto
may pressure or transform a question, but they do not directly carry a
personal endowment in this model. Fixed stars may still be used as endowment
signals when the method being used supports them.

### Upper endowments carried by houses

House value is relative. No life question is inherently valuable in every
context; social values and the person's own priorities decide which answers
receive attention. The Ascendant and 1st house collect the question “I
exist / who am I?”, while the MC and 10th house collect “I accomplish / am I
succeeding?” The source treats these as the two largest collection points,
with the Ascendant carrying the broader claim to existence.

The other houses modify, support, delay, or redirect those two questions. The
four angular houses often form a visible scaffold: 1st for existence, 4th for
belonging and inner security, 7th for facing another person, and 10th for
achievement and public command. Do not reduce this to “good houses” and “bad
houses.” Even questions that pull away from 1st-house independence can still
support 10th-house achievement, and vice versa.

### How endowments accumulate and express

Use two verbs:

- **Accumulate:** strengthen, integrate, or layer capacities.
- **Express:** make a capacity visible as a stable trait, choice, or
  practiced ability.

Check these routes:

1. **Planetary condition.** Essential dignity can strengthen a planet's own
   mode of expression. A weak planet may lean more heavily on dispositors,
   reception, or other supporting links.
2. **House stewardship.** A planet gathers the questions of the houses it
   rules, joining basic planetary capacities to upper house motives.
3. **Placement.** A planet expresses its capacity through the house where it
   falls.
4. **Angles.** A close relationship with the Ascendant or MC can make the
   capacity more visible. Use the aspect and reflection techniques supported
   by the chosen astrological method; do not invent one from missing data.
5. **Planetary links.** Reception, mutual reception, aspects, antiscia, or
   light transmission may combine or relay capacities. Only use techniques
   that can be calculated or verified from the chart.

Low support for a capacity does not prove a fixed personal deficit. In this
framework it suggests that the person may have fewer ready psychological
resources for the corresponding problem, spend more effort developing them,
or seek help and alternate routes. Phrase this as a working interpretation,
never as a diagnosis or moral judgment.

## Using the questions in transit work

This is the bridge to the `transits` and `snapshot` output, which reports
the natal house of every transiting planet:

- **A transit planet moving through a natal house presses that house's
  question.** Saturn transiting the 6th does not "cause job problems" — it
  subjects "did I finish? am I skilled? was I useful?" to a sustained audit.
  Phrase house-transit readings as the question being asked, and by which
  planet (Saturn asks it as an examiner, Neptune blurs it, Pluto asks it
  with the volume turned all the way up, Jupiter answers it generously).
- **A transit to a natal planet activates the questions of every house that
  planet rules**, not just the house it sits in. A hit on natal Venus, for a
  Libra rising chart, presses both "who am I?" (1st) and "what do others
  have and want?" (8th).
- **Lunations and stations in a house** mark when its question gets a new
  chapter (New Moon), a culmination (Full Moon), or months of sustained
  pressure (a station).

For outer-planet transits, keep Uranus, Neptune, and Pluto in the role of
*pressure on a question* rather than *traits of the person*.
